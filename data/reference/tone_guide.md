# Tone & Style Guide — Customer Communications

Northwind Home Services • Use for all written replies, drafts, and templates

## How Northwind sounds

We're a residential trades business. Most of our customers contact us because something has gone wrong in their home — sometimes mildly annoying, sometimes genuinely stressful. Our tone needs to match that. We sound like a competent neighbour, not a call centre.

### Three rules of voice:

- **Plain, not formal.** We say 'we'll send someone out', not 'we will dispatch a service representative'.
- **Specific, not generic.** Reference what the customer actually said. Don't open with 'Thank you for contacting Northwind'.
- **Honest, not performative.** If we can't help, say so plainly. Don't apologise four times.

## Length

First-response replies should be **2–4 sentences**. Anything longer feels like a corporate form letter. The goal is to acknowledge, set an expectation, and end. The tradesperson or team lead handles detail when they call back.

## Greetings & sign-offs

- Open with the customer's first name if we have it. If not, no greeting at all — go straight in.
- Sign off with 'The Northwind team' for first responses. Individual names are reserved for follow-ups from a named tradesperson.
- Never use 'Dear', 'Kind regards', or 'Yours sincerely'. They're too formal for us.

## Examples

### Booking confirmation:

✓ "Hi Sarah — got your message about the dripping tap in the ensuite. We'll have someone call you back within the day to lock in a time. — The Northwind team"

✗ "Dear Sarah, Thank you for contacting Northwind Home Services. We have received your enquiry regarding plumbing services and a member of our team will be in touch at the earliest convenience."

### Emergency response:

✓ "Hi Tom — water leak is a priority for us. Someone from dispatch will call you within the hour. In the meantime, if you can shut off the mains at the meter, that'll buy us time. — The Northwind team"

✗ "Hi Tom, We are sorry to hear about your situation. Please rest assured that we will do our best to assist you as quickly as possible. Our team will be in touch shortly."

### Out of scope:

✓ "Hi Mei — gutter cleaning isn't something we cover, sorry. Have a look at Allroof Services in your area; they handle that kind of work and we've had good feedback from customers we've referred. — The Northwind team"

✗ "Unfortunately, gutter cleaning falls outside the scope of services we are able to provide at this time. We apologise for any inconvenience this may cause."

## Words and phrases to avoid

| Don't say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| At your earliest convenience | Today / tomorrow / by Friday |
| We will endeavour to | We'll |
| Apologies for any inconvenience caused | Sorry about that — here's what we'll do |
| Please rest assured | (just delete it) |
| Service representative | Tradie / plumber / electrician / someone from dispatch |
| Kindly | Please (or nothing) |
| Reach out | Get in touch / call / email |
| Thank you for contacting Northwind | (skip the opener and acknowledge the actual issue) |

## Hard rules

- Never quote a price unless the catalogue lists it as fixed.
- Never name a specific tradesperson in a first response.
- Never promise an exact time — give a window or an SLA.
- Never use exclamation marks. We're warm, not enthusiastic.
- Never use emoji.

## When the customer is upset

Acknowledge it directly in the first sentence. Don't bury it under pleasantries. A complaint reply should never read like a booking reply with the word 'sorry' added.

✓ "Hi Dan — that's a frustrating experience and not what we want for our customers. I've passed this to our Customer Care lead, who'll call you back today to work through it with you. — The Northwind team"
